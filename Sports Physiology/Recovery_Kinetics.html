Bio-Mathematical Recovery Models

Understanding how an athlete depletes and restores their anaerobic work capacity ($W'$) is critical for a sport like Canoe Slalom, characterized by 90-110 second maximal efforts with highly variable power outputs. This section contrasts the standard Skiba model with the Bartram modification.

Select Model Context

The Skiba Approach

The original Skiba model assumes $W'$ (anaerobic work capacity) reconstitutes exponentially during recovery periods below Critical Power (CP). It suggests a relatively constant time constant ($\tau_W$) regardless of the preceding work's intensity, treating the body like a simple battery.

Slalom Implication: Useful for general steady-state paddling, but may overestimate recovery speed during the intense, stochastic sprint blocks seen in upstream gate combinations.

Simulated: 60s maximal effort (gate sequence) followed by active recovery below CP.

Session Typologies & Recovery Timelines

Different training stimuli generate distinct fatigue profiles. A Lactate Tolerance session creates massive peripheral fatigue, while Alactic Production stresses the CNS. Select a session type below to view its physiological toll and required recovery curve.

Lactate Tolerance (Glycolytic Capacity)

⏱️ E.g., 4-6 x 90s full course runs (1:3 Work:Rest)

Designed to mimic the exact duration and physiological demands of a competitive slalom run. Causes extreme hydrogen ion ($H^+$) accumulation and rapid drop in muscle pH. Drives massive metabolic fatigue and severely depletes localized glycogen stores in the upper body musculature.

Practical Application Matrix

Synthesizing theoretical models (Bartram/Skiba) and session fatigue profiles into a weekly microcycle planning framework for Canoe Slalom.

🧠

1. Assess the Drain

Apply the Bartram modification when planning intervals. If prescribing Severe intensity (>CP), recognize that $W'$ reconstitution will be up to 30% slower than standard mathematical models predict. Do not prescribe rest purely on a 1:1 ratio if the effort is maximal.

🩸

2. Target the Modality

Match recovery strategy to the primary physiological toll.

Metabolic fatigue (Lactate Tolerance) requires active flush (light spinning/paddling).
CNS fatigue (Lactate Production/Sprints) requires absolute passive rest and sleep optimization.

📅

3. Structure the Microcycle

Never stack severe $W'$ depleting sessions (like full 90s race simulations) back-to-back. Ensure a minimum of 48 hours between Lactate Tolerance blocks. Insert technical/tactical sessions (below CP) on intermediate days to allow slow-phase exponential recovery.
