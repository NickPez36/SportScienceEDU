Cross-Domain Application Matrix

How these physiological landmarks dictate training focus across varying time/intensity barriers. Canoe Slalom primarily resides in the demands of the 'Middle Distance' column.

S

Sprint / Power
< 60 seconds

  • VLamax: MAXIMIZE. The engine's capacity to generate rapid glycolytic energy is paramount.
  • CP / LT2: Secondary. Base needed for recovery between bouts, but threshold is rarely crossed in competition.
  • MAOD: High reliance on finite W' (anaerobic capacity).
M

Middle / Slalom
1 to 5 minutes

  • VO2 Max: CRITICAL. The ceiling must be high to support intense aerobic contribution during the run.
  • MAOD & W': CRITICAL. Slalom requires repeated near-max bursts (upstream gates). W' must be large and replenish quickly.
  • VLamax Balance: Needs to be high enough for explosive power, but low enough not to flood the system with lactate early in a 90s run.
E

Endurance
> 10 minutes

  • LT1 / LT2 (CP): MAXIMIZE. Pushing the threshold curve to the right is the primary determinant of race pace.
  • VLamax: MINIMIZE. Lower glycolytic power spares glycogen and reduces lactate accumulation at sub-max speeds.
  • VO2 Max: Important prerequisite, but 'fractional utilization' (LT2 as a % of VO2max) wins medals.
